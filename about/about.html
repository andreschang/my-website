About this site

This site primarily documents my intermittent creative practice. However, I have updated this section to reflect my primary occupation with sustainability research and practice.

Research statement

My research interests are broad but generally involve energy transitions, extractivism, and transnational sustainability initiatives. I take seriously the distinct goals, accountability relationships, and enforcement capabilities available to public and private actors, and the interactions between them that shape governance. With a preference for quantitative and mixed methodologies, I aspire to conduct research that improves understanding of (1) the shape and evolution of multi-actor climate and critical minerals governance spheres and (2) the social and environmental outcomes they are linked to "on the ground." Although the scope of these issues is global, I am developing a specific interest in extractive industries in Peru.

Professional background

Between 2018 and 2022, I worked on the technical team of the Science Based Targets initiative, a pioneering standard-setter and validator of corporate climate targets. Due partly to my undergraduate training in the physical sciences, my assignments typically involved the "translation" of global goals into cross-sector frameworks. Working closely with the initiative's executive body and scientific advisors, I led technical aspects of key projects such as the Corporate Net-Zero Standard (V1.0) and became the initiative's first Research Manager.

I changed paths in 2022 when I accepted a Senior Research Specialist position at Greenpeace USA. Within my new role, I carried out research supportive of campaigns to halt the build-out of U.S. liquefied natural gas infrastructure and defend the right to protest from corporate-funded attacks. The dynamic nature of advocacy, as well as the breadth of social and environmental issues confronted by Greenpeace, meant that I tracked a broad range of issues from the local to the global scale. My projects varied but typically involved a focus on environmental justice, political analysis, corporate research, and/or quantifying emissions.

Currently I am pursuing a Master of Environment and Sustainability at the University of Toronto.
