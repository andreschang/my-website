Exactly Where You Had Fallen

Two research documents with audio, mixed media. 2019.

I spoke to my parents about their memories of childhood, resurfacing formative experiences and impressions of place, weather, and the natural environment. In an attempt to reenact these personal histories, I synthesized hundreds of worlds using OCR Tools, a climate modeling software that I developed in 2018. I combed through each scenario, parsing key fields such as snowfall, temperature, and humidity as a speculative approach to identifying my parents’ memories in simulated time and space.

The documents are laser-printed on translucent vellum and annotated in white charcoal. They depict Wynnewood, PA (1968–1972) and Lima, Peru (1966–1973) dozens of times over. My handwriting spills across pages, in response to shared memories. The audio includes an excerpt of “Indian Creek with Neighbor Boy” by Robin Rosen Chang.
